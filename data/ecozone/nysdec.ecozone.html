Description:
Abstract:
Boundaries of the Ecological Regions of New York State, taken from Will et. al. (1982) and Dickinson (1983).
Purpose:
A polygon data set to be used in GIS applications showing the ecological regions of New York State.
Supplemental_Information:
1. In compiling a consolidated map for ecozones of New York State, John Ozard grouped 16 northern zones into three data management units labeled J, K, and L. These units were never intended to be major zones but were grouped simply to facilitate storage in a database.

2. In compiling a consolidated map for ecozones of New York State, John Ozard reconciled differences between the mappings of Will et al. and Dickinson. Additional information on the creation of a consolidated ecological zone map from the sources of Will et al. and Dickinson is available from the Habitat Inventory Unit, New York State Department of Environmental Conservation, 625 Broadway, Albany, NY 12233-4754.

Process_Date: 19901214
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
The MINOR item in the PAT was coded for polygons in the major zones F, G, H, and I (this had not been done, presumably because there are no subzones within these zones). For these polygons the alpha-numeric portion of the MINOR item was coded with the zone letter plus "01", and the proper name portion was coded the same as the major zone. The redifined item MINOR_NUM and MINOR_DESC were created to allow these two components of the MINOR item to be displayed separately. The zone coding of Governors and Roosevelt Islands were changed from Coastal Lowlands to Manhattan Hills, based on the bedrock geology (as depicted on the NYS Education Department's 1970 Geologic Map of NYS) and the zone descriptions in Dickinson 1983. These changes were completed on 8/16/96.

Attribute_Label: MAJOR
Attribute_Definition:
Major zones. Dickinson divided the southern and western part of the state into 9 major zones labeled A through I. Ozard aggregated the northern part of the state into 3 "zones" labeled J through L for data management purposes only. These 3 northern "major zones" have no ecological meaning. SEE SUPPLEMENTAL INFORMATION 1.
Attribute_Definition_Source: Dickinson and Ozard
Attribute_Domain_Values:
Unrepresentable_Domain:
This item contains the word "ZONE", a letter designation for the zone, and the major zone name for the southern and western zones derived from Dickenson.
Attribute:
Attribute_Label: MINOR
Attribute_Definition:
Minor zones. Dickinson further divided the southern and western part of the state into 22 subzones. In his compilation, Ozard considered these subzones hierarchically equivalent to the 25 northern zones of Will et. al. These zones, consolidated to number 40 by Ozard, are recorded in this item. SEE SUPPLEMENTAL INFORMATION 1.
